Strategic AI Implementation Assessment

Not all problems require AI solutions. This tool helps you identify where AI adds genuine value, which type fits best, and where traditional methods might be more effective.

What You'll Discover:

  • Whether your problem benefits from AI
  • Which type of AI solution is most appropriate
  • If automation or augmentation is the better approach
  • Potential risks and organizational readiness factors

Question 1: Problem Characteristics

Does your problem involve analyzing large amounts of data, identifying patterns, or handling repetitive tasks?

Examples of AI-suitable problems:

  • Large data analysis: Processing thousands of customer reviews, financial transactions, or sensor readings
  • Pattern identification: Detecting fraud, predicting equipment failures, or recognizing images
  • Repetitive tasks: Data entry, email classification, or routine customer inquiries

Question 2: Traditional Solutions

Can traditional methods (e.g., rule-based systems, manual processes, simple automation) solve your problem effectively?

Traditional methods include:

  • Rule-based systems: If-then logic, decision tables, workflow automation
  • Manual processes: Human expertise, established procedures
  • Simple automation: Scheduled tasks, basic data processing scripts

Consider: Is the problem well-defined with clear rules, or does it require learning and adaptation?

Question 3: Problem Scope

Is your problem specific and well-defined, or broad and complex?

Problem types:

Specific & Well-defined
  • Image recognition
  • Fraud detection
  • Predictive maintenance
  • Speech-to-text
Broad & Complex
  • Strategic decision-making
  • Content generation
  • Creative problem-solving
  • Multi-domain analysis

Question 4: Human Involvement

Would automating the entire task be more efficient, or is human oversight still valuable?

Consider these factors:

Full Automation
  • High-volume, routine tasks
  • Well-defined success criteria
  • Low risk of errors
  • 24/7 operation needed

Examples: Chatbots, automated data entry, spam filtering

Human Augmentation
  • Complex decision-making
  • Creative or strategic tasks
  • High-stakes outcomes
  • Need for explanation/accountability

Examples: AI-assisted diagnosis, investment recommendations, content creation

Question 5: Risk Assessment

Are there significant ethical, privacy, or transparency concerns with your use case?

Key concerns to evaluate:

  • Data sensitivity: Personal information, financial data, health records
  • Potential bias: Unfair treatment of certain groups or demographics
  • Transparency needs: Ability to explain AI decisions to stakeholders
  • Regulatory compliance: GDPR, HIPAA, industry-specific regulations
  • Reputational risk: Impact of AI errors on brand and trust

Question 6: Organizational Readiness

Does your organization have the necessary data infrastructure and skilled personnel to implement AI?

Readiness checklist:

Data Infrastructure
  • Quality, accessible data
  • Data governance processes
  • Scalable storage and computing
  • Integration capabilities
Human Resources
  • AI/ML expertise (internal or external)
  • Data science capabilities
  • Change management support
  • Executive sponsorship

AI Not Recommended

Recommended Actions:

  • Evaluate manual processes or simple automation

  • Focus on process optimization before technology
  • Reassess if problem characteristics change significantly

  • Create transparency and explainability protocols
